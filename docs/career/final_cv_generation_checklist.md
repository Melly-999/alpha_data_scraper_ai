## ATS-Friendly Formatting Checklist

- Use standard headings: Summary, Skills, Experience, Projects, Education, Languages, Certifications.
- Avoid tables in the final CV if ATS parsing is important.
- Avoid icons, text boxes, image-only sections and complex columns.
- Use simple bullet points and consistent date formatting.
- Put role titles and company/project names in text, not images.
- Keep links as plain text or simple hyperlinks.
- Use standard fonts such as Calibri, Aptos, Arial or similar.
- Export PDF from DOCX after reviewing layout.

- Verify spelling and language consistency.

## PDF Export Checklist

- Export from the reviewed DOCX version.
- Open the PDF and inspect every page manually.
- Check that links are clickable where useful.
- Check that layout did not break after export.
- Check that no private notes, comments or hidden review marks are visible.
- Use PDF for applications unless the employer specifically asks for DOCX.

## Privacy Checklist Before Sending

- Contact details are intentional and correct.
- No full private address unless explicitly needed.
- No private career notes are attached.
- No private source DOCX/PDF placeholders are attached by mistake.
- No phone/email appears in public repo docs.
- No private CV file is copied into the repository.
- No screenshots, videos or other media are bundled with the CV unless manually reviewed.
